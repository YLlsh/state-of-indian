Sikkim – The Land of Mystic Splendor

Sikkim is a serene and picturesque state in northeastern India, known for its snow-capped mountains, Buddhist monasteries, biodiversity, and eco-tourism. It is home to Kangchenjunga, the third highest peak in the world.

Capital: Gangtok

Population: Approximately 0.7 million

Location:
Located in northeastern India, bordered by Nepal, Bhutan, Tibet (China), and West Bengal.

Famous Tourist Places:
Gangtok, Tsomgo Lake, Nathula Pass, Yumthang Valley, Pelling

Culture / Festivals:
Losar, Pang Lhabsol, Saga Dawa, Lepcha and Bhutia traditions

Main Industries: Tourism, Floriculture, Organic Farming, Handicrafts

Tamil Nadu is a culturally rich state in southern India known for its ancient temples, classical arts, Dravidian architecture, and vibrant traditions. It is one of India's most industrialized states.

Capital: Chennai

Population: Approximately 78 million

Location:
Located in southern India, bordered by Kerala, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and the Bay of Bengal.

Famous Tourist Places:
Madurai Meenakshi Temple, Mahabalipuram, Ooty, Kanyakumari, Thanjavur

Culture / Festivals:
Pongal, Bharatanatyam, Tamil New Year, Carnatic Music, Jallikattu

Main Industries: Automobiles, Textiles, IT, Agriculture, Electronics

Tamil Nadu – Land of Temples

Telangana – Gateway to the Deccan

Telangana is a newly formed state in southern India, known for its rich history, IT industry, historic monuments, and vibrant culture. It was carved out of Andhra Pradesh in 2014.

Capital: Hyderabad

Population: Approximately 40 million

Location:
Located in southern India, bordered by Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh.

Famous Tourist Places:
Charminar, Golconda Fort, Ramappa Temple, Hussain Sagar Lake, Warangal

Culture / Festivals:
Bathukamma, Bonalu, Deccan cuisine, Perini dance

Main Industries: IT, Pharmaceuticals, Agriculture, Textiles, Biotechnology

Tripura is a beautiful northeastern state known for its lush green hills, royal palaces, and tribal culture. It has a unique cultural blend of Bengali and indigenous traditions.

Capital: Agartala

Population: Approximately 4 million

Location:
Located in northeast India, bordered by Bangladesh on three sides, and Assam and Mizoram.

Famous Tourist Places:
Ujjayanta Palace, Neermahal, Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary, Unakoti

Culture / Festivals:
Garia Puja, Durga Puja, Tripuri, Reang, and Bengali traditions

Main Industries: Handicrafts, Rubber, Bamboo Products, Agriculture

Tripura – Land of Palaces and Hills

Uttar Pradesh – Heartland of India

Uttar Pradesh is India’s most populous state, rich in history, religion, and cultural heritage. It is home to sacred cities, Mughal architecture, and significant events in Indian mythology.

Capital: Lucknow

Population: Over 240 million

Location:
Located in northern India, bordered by Uttarakhand, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, and others.

Famous Tourist Places:
Taj Mahal, Varanasi, Ayodhya, Sarnath, Fatehpur Sikri

Culture / Festivals:
Holi, Diwali, Ram Leela, Kathak dance, Awadhi cuisine

Main Industries: Textiles, Leather, Agriculture, Sugar, Handicrafts

Uttarakhand, often referred to as Devbhumi (Land of Gods), is a Himalayan state with sacred rivers, pilgrimage sites, and natural beauty. It is a top destination for trekking and spiritual tourism.

Capital: Dehradun

Population: Approximately 11 million

Location:
Located in northern India, bordered by Himachal Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, and Tibet (China).

Famous Tourist Places:
Haridwar, Rishikesh, Nainital, Kedarnath, Valley of Flowers

Culture / Festivals:
Kumbh Mela, Nanda Devi Raj Jat, Kumaoni and Garhwali traditions

Main Industries: Tourism, Hydropower, Horticulture, Herbal Products

Uttarakhand – Devbhumi, Land of Gods

West Bengal – Cultural Capital of India

West Bengal is a culturally rich state in eastern India, known for its literature, arts, festivals, and intellectual traditions. It stretches from the Himalayas to the Bay of Bengal.

Capital: Kolkata

Population: Approximately 100 million

Location:
Located in eastern India, bordered by Bangladesh, Odisha, Jharkhand, Bihar, Sikkim, and Bhutan.

Famous Tourist Places:
Sundarbans, Darjeeling, Victoria Memorial, Kalimpong, Digha

Culture / Festivals:
Durga Puja, Rabindra Jayanti, Baul Music, Bengali Cinema and Cuisine

Main Industries: Jute, Tea, Petrochemicals, IT, Literature, Arts
