Sikkim – The Land of Mystic Splendor

Sikkim is a serene and picturesque state in northeastern India, known for its snow-capped mountains, Buddhist monasteries, biodiversity, and eco-tourism. It is home to Kangchenjunga, the third highest peak in the world.

Capital: Gangtok

Population: Approximately 0.7 million

Location:
Located in northeastern India, bordered by Nepal, Bhutan, Tibet (China), and West Bengal.

Famous Tourist Places:
Gangtok, Tsomgo Lake, Nathula Pass, Yumthang Valley, Pelling

Culture / Festivals:
Losar, Pang Lhabsol, Saga Dawa, Lepcha and Bhutia traditions

Main Industries: Tourism, Floriculture, Organic Farming, Handicrafts

Tamil Nadu is a culturally rich state in southern India known for its ancient temples, classical arts, Dravidian architecture, and vibrant traditions. It is one of India's most industrialized states.

Capital: Chennai

Population: Approximately 78 million

Location:
Located in southern India, bordered by Kerala, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and the Bay of Bengal.

Famous Tourist Places:
Madurai Meenakshi Temple, Mahabalipuram, Ooty, Kanyakumari, Thanjavur

Culture / Festivals:
Pongal, Bharatanatyam, Tamil New Year, Carnatic Music, Jallikattu

Main Industries: Automobiles, Textiles, IT, Agriculture, Electronics

Tamil Nadu – Land of Temples

Manipur – The Jewel of India

Manipur is a northeastern state of India, known for its scenic landscapes, classical Manipuri dance, and diverse tribal heritage.

Capital: Imphal

Population: Approximately 3.1 million

Location:
Located in northeastern India, bordered by Nagaland, Mizoram, Assam, and Myanmar.

Famous Tourist Places:
Loktak Lake, Kangla Fort, Keibul Lamjao National Park, Ukhrul

Culture / Festivals:
Yaoshang, Lai Haraoba, Manipuri classical dance, tribal festivals

Main Industries: Handloom, Handicrafts, Agriculture, Tourism

Mizoram is a mountainous state in northeastern India known for its vibrant tribal culture, dense forests, and scenic beauty. It is one of the most literate states in India.

Capital: Aizawl

Population: Approximately 1.2 million

Location:
Located in northeastern India, bordered by Myanmar, Bangladesh, Tripura, Assam, and Manipur.

Famous Tourist Places:
Aizawl, Reiek, Vantawng Falls, Phawngpui Hills

Culture / Festivals:
Chapchar Kut, Mim Kut, Pawl Kut, Mizo dance and music

Main Industries: Handloom, Bamboo, Horticulture, Tourism

Mizoram – The Land of the Hill People

Meghalaya – The Abode of Clouds

Meghalaya is a beautiful hill state in northeastern India, famous for its lush landscapes, high rainfall, waterfalls, caves, and tribal culture.

Capital: Shillong

Population: Approximately 3.3 million

Location:
Located in northeastern India, it shares borders with Assam and Bangladesh.

Famous Tourist Places:
Cherrapunji, Mawsynram, Dawki, Living Root Bridges, Nohkalikai Falls

Culture / Festivals:
Wangala, Shad Suk Mynsiem, Nongkrem Dance, Khasi and Garo traditions

Main Industries: Agriculture, Tourism, Handicrafts, Horticulture

Nagaland is a culturally rich state in northeastern India, known for its tribal diversity, vibrant festivals, and scenic hills. It is famous for the Hornbill Festival and traditional craftsmanship.

Capital: Kohima

Population: Approximately 2.3 million

Location:
Located in northeastern India, bordered by Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, and Myanmar.

Famous Tourist Places:
Kohima, Dzukou Valley, Mokokchung, Mon, Tuophema Village

Culture / Festivals:
Hornbill Festival, Moatsu, Sekrenyi, tribal heritage and dances

Nagaland – The Land of Festivals

Odisha – The Soul of Incredible India

Odisha is a culturally rich state located on the eastern coast of India, known for its ancient temples, classical dance, tribal communities, and beautiful beaches. It has a deep connection with Indian history, especially the Kalinga War.

Capital: Bhubaneswar

Population: Approximately 46 million

Location:
Located on the eastern coast of India, it is bordered by West Bengal, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, and Andhra Pradesh.

Famous Tourist Places:
Puri Jagannath Temple, Konark Sun Temple, Chilika Lake, Bhitarkanika National Park, Lingaraj Temple

Culture / Festivals:
Rath Yatra, Odissi Dance, Nuakhai, Chhau Dance, Tribal Arts

Main Industries: Mining, Steel, Power, Handicrafts, Tourism, Agriculture
