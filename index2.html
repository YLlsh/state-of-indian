Chhattisgarh – The Rice Bowl of India

Chhattisgarh is a mineral-rich state in central India known for its forests, waterfalls, and tribal culture. It offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and economic resources.

Capital: Raipur

Population: Approximately 30 million

Location:
Located in central India, it shares borders with states like Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Telangana, and Jharkhand.

Famous Tourist Places:
Chitrakote Falls, Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary, Bhoramdeo Temple, Mainpat

Culture / Festivals:
Bastar Dussehra, Hareli, tribal dances like Raut Nacha and Panthi

Main Industries: Mining, Power, Steel, Agriculture

Bihar is a historic state in eastern India, known for its ancient universities, spiritual heritage, and cultural legacy. It played a central role in the development of Buddhism and Jainism.

Capital: Patna

Population: Approximately 125 million

Location:
Located in eastern India, it borders Nepal, Uttar Pradesh, Jharkhand, and West Bengal.

Famous Tourist Places:
Bodh Gaya, Nalanda, Rajgir, Vaishali, Patna Sahib

Culture / Festivals:
Chhath Puja, Sonepur Mela, Madhubani art, Maithili and Bhojpuri folk traditions

Main Industries: Agriculture, Handloom, Leather, Tourism

Bihar – Cradle of Ancient India

Haryana – The Land of Milk and Glory

Haryana is a prosperous state in northern India known for its agricultural success, historical significance, and rapid industrialization. It played a key role in the Mahabharata era and continues to be a major contributor to India's economy.

Capital: Chandigarh (shared with Punjab)

Population: Approximately 29 million

Location:
Located in northern India, it borders Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, Rajasthan, and Uttar Pradesh.

Famous Tourist Places:
Kurukshetra, Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary, Morni Hills, Surajkund

Culture / Festivals:
Surajkund Mela, Teej, Phalgun Mela, Haryanvi music and dance

Main Industries: Agriculture, Dairy, Automotive, IT, Textiles

Goa is a coastal state in western India renowned for its beaches, Portuguese heritage, vibrant nightlife, and seafood. It attracts tourists from all over the world for its relaxed atmosphere and scenic beauty.

Capital: Panaji

Population: Approximately 1.6 million

Location:
Situated on the western coast of India, bordered by the Arabian Sea, Maharashtra, and Karnataka.

Famous Tourist Places:
Calangute Beach, Fort Aguada, Basilica of Bom Jesus, Dudhsagar Falls

Culture / Festivals:
Goa Carnival, Shigmo, Christmas, Konkani and Portuguese influences

Main Industries: Tourism, Mining, Fishing, Pharmaceuticals

Goa – Pearl of the Orient

Himachal Pradesh – Abode of Snow

Himachal Pradesh is a scenic state in northern India, known for its Himalayan landscapes, hill stations, and adventure tourism. It offers serene beauty and a peaceful lifestyle.

Capital: Shimla (Summer), Dharamshala (Winter)

Population: Approximately 7.4 million

Location:
Located in northern India, it borders Punjab, Haryana, Uttarakhand, and Jammu & Kashmir.

Famous Tourist Places:
Shimla, Manali, Dharamshala, Kullu, Spiti Valley.

Culture / Festivals:
Kullu Dussehra, Losar, Phulaich, traditional Himachali folk music.

Main Industries: Tourism, Agriculture, Horticulture, Hydropower.

Jharkhand is a mineral-rich state in eastern India, known for its forests, waterfalls, and tribal culture. It plays a key role in India's mining and industrial sectors.

Capital: Ranchi

Population: Approximately 39 million

Location:
Located in eastern India, bordered by Bihar, West Bengal, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, and Uttar Pradesh.

Famous Tourist Places:
Netarhat, Betla National Park, Dassam Falls, Hundru Falls.

Culture / Festivals:
Sarhul, Karma, Sohrai, tribal music and dances.

Main Industries: Mining (coal, iron ore), Steel, Power, Forestry.

Jharkhand – The Land of Forests

Karnataka – The Silicon Valley of India

Karnataka is a dynamic state in southern India known for its technological hubs, rich cultural heritage, classical music, and historical temples. It balances modern development with ancient traditions.

Capital: Bengaluru

Population: Approximately 70 million

Location:
Located in southern India, bordered by Goa, Maharashtra, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, and Kerala.

Famous Tourist Places:
Mysore Palace, Hampi, Coorg, Badami, Gokarna.

Culture / Festivals:
Dasara, Ugadi, Yakshagana, Carnatic music, Sandalwood crafts.

Main Industries: IT, Biotechnology, Aerospace, Silk, Agriculture, Education.
