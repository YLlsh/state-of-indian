Arunachal Pradesh – The Land of the Rising Sun

Arunachal Pradesh is the northeasternmost state of India, known for its pristine natural beauty, diverse tribal culture, and mountainous terrain. It is the first Indian state to greet the sunrise.

Capital: Itanagar

Population: Approximately 1.6 million

Location:
Arunachal Pradesh is located in the far northeastern part of India, sharing borders with Bhutan, China, and Myanmar.

Famous Tourist Places:
Tawang Monastery, Ziro Valley, Namdapha National Park, Bomdila, Sela Pass.

Culture / Festivals:
Losar, Dree Festival, Solung, Nyokum, Si-Donyi.

Main Industries: Agriculture, Handicrafts, Horticulture, Hydropower, Tourism.

Gujarat is a vibrant state located in western India, known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and strong industrial base. It is the home state of Mahatma Gandhi and offers a blend of tradition and modern development.

Capital: Gandhinagar

Population: Approximately 80 million

Location:
Gujarat is in western India, bordered by the Arabian Sea, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, and Maharashtra

Famous Tourist Places:
Gir National Park , Somnath Temple, Dwarkadhish Temple, and the Statue of Unity

Culture / Festivals:
Gujarat is famous for Garba and Dandiya Raas dance forms, Navratri festival, colorful handicrafts

Main Industries: Key sectors: textiles, diamonds, chemicals, and ports.

Gujarat – The Jewel of Western India

Assam – Gateway to the Northeast

Assam is a scenic and culturally rich state in northeastern India, renowned for its tea gardens, wildlife sanctuaries, and vibrant traditions. It serves as the entry point to the rest of the northeastern region of India and is known for its natural beauty and heritage.

Capital: Jaipur

Population: Approximately 80 million

Location:
Northwestern India, bordering Pakistan and Indian states like Punjab, Gujarat, and Madhya Pradesh.

Famous Tourist Places:
Jaipur, Udaipur, Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, Mount Abu

Culture / Festivals:
Teej, Desert Festival, Ghoomar Dance, Dal Baati Churma

Main Industries: Tourism, Handicrafts, Mining, Agriculture

Punjab, known as the "Land of Five Rivers", is a vibrant northern Indian state rich in history, culture, and agriculture.

Capital:Chandigarh

Population: Approximately 30 million

Location:
Punjab is located in the northwestern part of India.

Famous Tourist Places:
Golden Temple, Wagah Border, Rock Garden in Chandigarh.

Culture / Festivals:
Bhangra dance, colorful festivals like Lohri, Baisakhi, and Gurpurab.

Main Industries:textiles, manufacturing, handicrafts, and agro-based industries.

Punjab – The Land of Five Rivers

Maharashtra – The Gateway to India's Heart

Maharashtra is one of India’s largest and most industrialized states, known for its economic power, cultural diversity, and historical landmarks. It is home to Mumbai, the financial capital of India.

Capital:Mumbai

Population: Approximately 125 million

Location:
Maharashtra is located in the western part of India.

Famous Tourist Places:
Gateway of India, Ajanta and Ellora Caves, Marine Drive, Shirdi, Lonavala.

Culture / Festivals:
Ganesh chaturthi, Gudi padwa, Diwali

Main Industries:Finance, IT, Automobiles, textiles, Film Industry, Agriculture.

Andhra Pradesh is known for its rich cultural heritage, classical dance, temples, and scenic coastlines. It plays a significant role in agriculture and has a rapidly growing economy.

Capital: Amaravati (Legislative), Visakhapatnam (Executive)

Population: Approximately 54 million

Location:
Andhra Pradesh is located in the southeastern part of India.

Famous Tourist Places:
Tirupati, Araku Valley, Vijayawada Kanaka Durga Temple, Amaravati Stupa, Borra Caves.

Culture / Festivals:
Ugadi, Sankranti, Rama Navami, Dasara.

Main Industries: Agriculture (Rice, Tobacco), Information Technology, Handloom, Pharmaceuticals, Marine Products.

Andhra Pradesh – The Rice Bowl of India

Rajasthan – Land of Kings

Rajasthan is a vibrant state in northwestern India, known for its royal heritage, desert landscapes, colorful festivals, and majestic forts and palaces. It is India’s largest state by area and rich in both history and culture.

Capital: Jaipur

Population: Approximately 80 million

Location:
Northwestern India, bordering Pakistan and Indian states like Punjab, Gujarat, and Madhya Pradesh.

Famous Tourist Places:
Jaipur, Udaipur, Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, Mount Abu

Culture / Festivals:
Teej, Desert Festival, Ghoomar Dance, Dal Baati Churma

Main Industries: Tourism, Handicrafts, Mining, Agriculture
